Chapter 1: The VoidGate Institution

Released in the archive.

It’s dark. It’s always dark in the City of Power, but this is a special kind of dark. This is the dark of the VoidGate Institution.

Plasma rests against one of the walls in the cell, feeling the cold metal against his back. It provides some respite from his exhaustion, but he can feel the way the metal seems to absorb his power, leaving behind a dangerous cold. The kind of cold one feels before their eyes close for the last time.

Plasma pushes away from the wall.

Across from him, Spiral and Oshin sit across from one another. They have their hands out in front of them, moving back and forth in some sort of pattern. Oshin brings one of his hands up, tapping Spiral’s wrist. His fangs glint in the dim light as he smiles. Spiral curses, putting her hand down.

“What’s that?” Plasma asks. His voice is rough from the days of silence.

“You’ve never played Squadron before?” Spiral asks.

“I’ve heard of it.”

“It’s a kid’s hand game.”

“I said I’ve heard of it. Who plays a kid’s game in the Void?”

There’s a movement in a corner of the cell. Raymond lifts his head, his eyes meeting Plasma’s.

“Let them play their game, Plasma. They aren’t disturbing you.”

So the four sit in this cell. They’re a cell amongst the hundreds. Distantly heard is the sound of footsteps on metal, of the guards patrolling on catwalks high above the cell block.

For now, it’s best to sleep.

~ ~ ~

There’s a loud shrieking noise as metal grates against metal, and one of the sides of the cell swings open.

The four squint as they’re bathed in a harsh neon light coming from the hall outside the cell.

As their eyes struggle to adjust, nine shadowy figures sharpen into focus in front of them.

Eight of them wear the VoidGuard uniform. Dense metallic plating covers their battery packs, and a neon blue energy sparks off of the rifles aimed into the cell.

Their faces are obscured by shining masks.

Raymond catches his own face staring back at him. He winks at his reflection.

The guard he’s looking at raises their gun in warning.

In the center of this firing squad stands a man.

“Stands” is generous.

He’s being held up by two of the guards, making it unclear if he’s conscious or not.

Plasma stares at him.

For a moment, there is quiet again, save for the hum of the rifles.

“You’ve got a new one.” The guard says, pushing the stranger into the cell.

“The cells are meant for four.” Plasma says.

“That’s not your decision.”

“That’s inhumane.” Spiral says.

“Submit a complaint, then.”

~ ~ ~

“I don’t know,” Purple says. “Weeks?”

“What the fuck are we supposed to do in that time?” Spiral says.

There’s a pause.

Some of them had been here for months, some for years, but now with the potential of escape looming so close, time seemed to slow.

~ ~ ~

“Do you have enough power yet?”

“No.”

~ ~ ~

“Now?”

“No.”

~ ~ ~

“Now?”

“Spiral, shut the fuck up.”

~ ~ ~

“I assume we’re going to need some sort of plan.” Oshin replies.

“We have a plan. He’s got a laser.” Spiral says.

Oshin rolls his eyes. “I assume there’s going to be more to that.”

“There’s definitely going to be more than that.” Plasma says.

“The prison’s a giant circle—”

“Boringgggggggg.” Purple says.

The light between his hands grows.

Brighter.

Hotter.

The cell fractures into chaos.

And then—

The beam fires.

~ ~ ~

The wall gives way.

A perfect hole burns through the metal.

Spiral is already moving.

“Let’s fucking GO.”

The others follow.

Plasma hesitates only for a moment, glancing back at the cell.

It looks smaller now.

Darker.

Then he runs.

~ ~ ~

“So what do we do…” Raymond asks.

Below them, the ocean churns.

Above them, the guards advance.

Closer.

Closer.

“Shit—DUCK.”
