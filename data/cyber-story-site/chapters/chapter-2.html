Chapter 1: The VoidGate Institution

Released in the archive.

It's dark. It's always dark in the City of Power, but this is a special kind of dark. This is the dark of the VoidGate Institution. Plasma rests against one of the walls in the cell, feeling the cold metal against his back. It provides some respite from his exhaustion, but he can feel the way the metal seems to absorb his power, leaving behind a dangerous cold. The kind of cold one feels before their eyes close for the last time. Plasma pushes away from the wall.

Across from him, Spiral and Oshin sit across from one another. They have their hands out in front of them, moving back and forth in some sort of pattern. Oshin brings one of his hands up, tapping Spiral's wrist. His fangs glint in the dim light as he smiles. Spiral curses, putting her hand down.

"What's that?" Plasma asks. His voice is rough from the days of silence. There's not much to say inside the Void. The prison steals speech from the prisoners as much as it does their power.

Spiral and Oshin look up. Their hands drop to their lap.

"You've never played Squadron before?" Spiral asks.

"I've heard of it." Plasma says.

"It's a kids hand game."

"I said I've heard of it. Who plays a kid's game in the Void?"

There's a movement in a corner of the cell. Raymond lifts his head, his eyes meeting Plasma's. They're weary, the same way every prisoner's eyes are, and yet even still there's a small twinkle of something within them.

"Let them play their game, Plasma. They aren't disturbing you." His voice carries a subtle twang, like a guitar note held for a moment too long. Like the cowboys from five hundred years ago. It's unclear whether the accent is forced.

So the four sit in this cell. They're a cell amongst the hundreds. Distantly heard is the sound of footsteps on metal, of the guards patrolling on catwalks high above the cell block. For now, it's best to sleep.

~ ~ ~

There's a loud shrieking noise as metal grates against metal, and one of the sides of the cell swings open. The four squint as they're bathed in a harsh neon light coming from the hall outside the cell. As their eyes struggle to adjust, nine shadowy figures sharpen into focus in front of them. Eight of them wear the VoidGuard uniform. Dense metallic plating covers their battery packs, and a neon blue energy sparks off of the rifles aimed into the cell. Their faces are obscured by shining masks. Raymond catches his own face staring back at him. He winks at his reflection. The guard he's looking at raises their gun in warning.

In the center of this firing squad stands a man. "Stands" is generous. He's being held up by two of the guards, making it unclear if he's conscious or not. His head rolls slightly as the guards pull him forward. He's wearing a thin shirt a size too large, and matching gray pants. The same uniform as the four in the cell. He's tall, muscular, with a shock of white hair that blends in with the harsh lighting above him. Plasma stares at him. For a moment, there is quiet again, save for the hum of the rifles.

"You've got a new one." The guard says, pushing the stranger into the cell.

"The cells are meant for four." Plasma says.

"That's not your decision." The guard responds. "The cells are meant for however many are placed inside."

"That's inhumane." Spiral says.

"Submit a complaint, then." The guard says. Plasma senses a hint of irony in the guard's voice, though through the robotic filter, it's hard to discern any emotion.

"Sir," A voice speaks up from the back, sauntering forward. The guards' guns focus on him.

"Stay back." The guard at the front shouts. Raymond stops.

"Sir I was just wondering if you could tell me the time–" Taking one step forward, Raymond is sharply cut off as the guard whips the base of their rifle into his jaw. Raymond collapses forward into the guard, who kicks him away, back into the cell.

"Leave him alone." Spiral says, getting to her feet. Half of the guns leave Raymond pointing towards her.

"Stay BACK. All of you."

Spiral freezes. Raymond groans, bringing a hand to his mouth. He swipes it across his lips, watching it come away streaked in blood.

"That's a warning for insubordination." The guard says.

The door slams, and the group is plunged back into darkness. The new inmate on the floor groans. Nobody looks at him. Raymond pushes himself up, bringing a hand to his mouth as if to cough.

"I got something." He says, the words muffled behind his hand. "I was able to pull a little bit from his power pack. It's something new. Some sort of control power?"

Spiral brings a hand to her mouth as well. "Control? Not gravity?"

"No, this is new. They've rotated the guards since last time. The gravity guard is gone." Raymond responds.

"Control's better than Gravity. We might be able to do something with that." Plasma says. "Do you think he noticed anything?"

"Definitely not. He was too busy hitting me in the face, and the rest were on Spiral. I don't think he even felt me touch his battery pack."

"How much were you able to get?" Oshin asks.

"Same amount as always," Raymond replies. "Each power uses it differently though, I don't know how much I'll get out of it this time."

The new inmate groans again. Louder. The four look at him. Their hands drop back to their sides.

"Is he ok?" Spiral asks. She turns to Plasma. "You're a doctor, right? Look at him."

"I was a combat medic. A long time ago. And I killed more people than I saved."

"Do you want a dead body in our cell?" Raymond asks.

Plasma flips him off, before dropping to one knee beside the body. He presses two fingers against the artery in the man's neck, feeling a strong pulse. "He's alive at least. I'm not seeing any injuries." Plasma's eyes trace the faded gray uniform, searching for any stains of blood, but finding none. If anything, the man in front of him seems perfectly healthy. The symptoms are shockingly familiar, and slowly, Plasma brings his hand up to the man's closed eyes, pushing up one eyelid.

The response is immediate. The small cell is filled with echoes of gasps and an "Oh shit" as the four step closer to the body. Instead of a dark pupil surrounded by white, the entirety of the man's eyes are stained a bright blue. The color seems to pulse and glow, shifting around the man's eye.

"Full drain." Plasma says, stepping back from the body grimly. "The guards gave us a dead body."

"He was groaning." Spiral says, stepping closer. "He's got a pulse."

"Sure, he's alive. In the most basic form of the word. He's burned every single bit of power in his body. What a fucking idiot."

"Plasma." Spiral says, glaring at him. "Have some respect."

"I'm not going to respect some fucking idiot who full-drained himself. You'd think he'd have been smart enough to stop when his battery pack ran out."

"You don't know his situation, man. Maybe he didn't have a choice." Raymond says.

"Well if you care so much, you can be the ones who feed him." Plasma says. "Or you can let him die. I'm not really a fan of how cramped our cell is getting anyway."

Silence falls as the four look between each other, then at the body. They're deliberating, weighing the pros and cons. The room already feels sizably smaller than it did before he was thrown in.

"So he's not going to wake up?" Spiral says. "You're sure?"

"It's a full-drain" Plasma responds. "His body has short circuited. Once you run out of power you lose the ability to generate it. You need power to generate more power and you need power to live. He's a corpse."

"Ok…" Oshin says. "Well there's no point in feeding a corpse so-"

The man on the ground coughs. Once. Twice. The four go silent, staring at him.

"Like I said," Plasma says. "Bodily reflexes. He's got the same amount of consciousness as–"

"Excuse me." The body on the ground says. "My name's Purple not he, and I'd greatly appreciate it if you shut the fuck up about me."

"Sorry?" Plasma says, his mind spinning. The bright blue eyes, it was a sure sign of a final power surge before a full-drain. He couldn't have been wrong. He'd seen this hundreds, not thousands of times before. The hundreds of full-drained bodies that littered the ground during the awakening… none of them ever moved again.

"Is that a reflex?" Spiral asks.

"No." Plasma says. "But it's not possible, that's not how it works."

"Can you shut the fuck up?" Purple asks, propping himself up on an elbow and squinting his neon blue eyes. "You're so loud."

"Your name's Purple? Like the color?" Raymond says.

"No. Like the vegetable." Purple says.

"Funny. You're funny. Great. Up until this point I've been the only funny one here." Raymond turns to face the others. "Guys he's funny. I like him." He points to Plasma. "That's Plasma, he's grumpy right now–he's always grumpy. He warms up to you after a bit, so don't worry too much. I think he also might still be getting over the whole you-should-be-dead thing. That right there's Spiral. The wolfman next to her is Oshin."

Purple looks at the canine humanoid sitting next to Spiral. Oshin smiles, lips peeling back to reveal sharp fangs. "Welcome." He says. "You still look like shit."

"Dog person." Purple says. "Can I pet him?"

Oshin's smile grows sharp. "If I can keep the hand."

"Whoa whoa whoa," Raymond interrupts. "We're nice. We're friendly. Calm down."

"Was he always a dog person?" Purple asks.

"He certainly hasn't felt like telling any of us in our time here." Raymond says. "I'll pay you twenty million credits if you can get him to answer. After we get out. Obviously."

"Raymond. Hand." Plasma snaps.

Raymond brings his hand to his mouth. "Right. They've got cameras on us. No microphones as far as we can tell. If you've got something to say, something you don't think the guards would take too kindly to, do it behind your hand."

Purple brings a hand to his mouth, mimicking him. "So you're planning on getting out of here?"

Raymond pauses for a moment. Deliberating. "Maybe. Why? Is that something you'd be open to?"

"Why wouldn't I be?" Purple responds.

"I don't know," Raymond says. "There's always those high and mighty prisoners who want to wait out their time. Repent."

"I've no reason to repent." Purple says. His stomach twists as he coughs the words out. The memories flash before him. The rubble, the flames. The child's hand beneath a pile of concrete. Oshin notices Purple's small frown, and smiles. It's a kind smile.

"It's the City of Power, Purple. Everyone has a reason to repent." He says.

"So?" Purple says, deciding to ignore that comment. "Do you have a way to get us out of here?"

"Give me a battery pack and I'll have us out of here within the hour." Spiral says. For the briefest moment, the hand in front of her mouth glows slightly. Just as quickly as it begins, it ends. "I'm not going to risk a full-drain over one escape attempt. We're not all like you, if I full-drain I'm out for the count."

"Yeah, no shit." Plasma rolls his eyes. "Give any of us a battery pack and we'll be out of here. I wonder why the first thing they did was confiscate our battery packs, Spiral. It's almost like… it's almost like they thought of that."

"Fuck off." Spiral says, flipping him off.

Purple reaches for the power inside of him. The vast reserve, usually partially filled, is empty, only a small fraction remaining. It's barely enough to keep his heart beating. But it's slowly accumulating, the way it always does. He looks up at the others. "I think I can help with that."

"Explain." Plasma says.

"The problem with the battery. I don't have that problem." Purple responds. "When I generate power… it doesn't stop."

"That doesn't make sense." Plasma says, examining him with a newfound curiosity.

"Nothing makes sense about that guy, he came back from a full-drain" Raymond says. "I don't find it difficult to believe he's got a battery within him." He turns to Purple. "Is it like an implant? No, they would've scanned you for implants…"

"No implants," Purple says, "It's just the way it's always been."

"You act like a generator and a battery. Fascinating. That would explain how you rebounded from a full-drain; your power generation isn't limited by anything, including itself." Plasma says. He steps up to Purple, examining his eyes carefully. "God, that's frightening. You would've been the apocalypse four hundred years ago."

"Four hundred years ago?" Purple says, stepping away from Plasma's inspection and batting his hand away.

"Before batteries. Back when there was nothing we could do with our power generation. You would've had the ability to harness an ability nobody else could. Thank god you weren't born in my time."

"Your time? You can't be more than forty." Purple says. "Right?"

"Older," Plasma says, smiling. "Just slightly."

"Ok, so you can get us out of here?" Oshin asks. Up until this point he'd been leaning against the wall, passively watching the conversation. Now he sits leaning forward, canine eyes focusing on Purple.

"Obviously not now," Plasma says, "He just rebounded, he barely has more power than we do. Is that right?"

One again, Purple feels the deep emptiness within his power reserves. "Right." He says.

"And your power? It better not be some fuckass sound manipulation." Oshin says. "Although if it is, feel free to deafen me. I want to sleep in peace without listening to these guys."

Purple looks around at the four expectant faces. Still strangers, all of them. He feels a twinge of annoyance. "You know, I barely know any of you." He says. "I feel like I've been giving most of the information here. Why don't you tell me first?"

Raymond sighs. "Understandable. Plasma, resurrection. Oshin, lightning and anthropomorphism. Don't ask me how that works, I don't know. Spiral, combustion. Me, it depends."

"It depends?" Purple asks.

Raymond smiles. "You asked for our powers, not for their explanations. It's your turn."

Purple rolls his eyes. "It doesn't take any form. I output the raw power itself."

Plasma lets out a sharp exhale. "And I thought Oshin and Raymond were strange. What a band of misfits."

"So you can definitely get us out of here, then." Spiral says. "Wait for your power to recharge, send a blast through one of the walls, and we skip out of here."

"The guards don't know about this ability of yours?" Raymond asks.

"How would they," Plasma says. "It's unheard of. How long is it going to take your power to regenerate?"

"I don't know," Purple says. "Weeks?"

"What the fuck are we supposed to do in that time?" Spiral says.

There's a pause. Some of them had been here for months, some for years, but now with the potential of escape looming so close, time seems to slow.

~ ~ ~

"Do you have enough power yet?"

"No."

~ ~ ~

"Now"

"No."

~ ~ ~

"Now?"

"Spiral, shut the fuck up," Oshin says, "You're driving us insane."

"I have every right to ask him!" Spiral replies.

It's unclear how much time has passed. Aside from Spiral's occasional interjections the cell has been silent. Before, any silence had been comfortable, a lull in conversation that could stretch days, even weeks. Now, the four fidget. Raymond's fingers trace patterns on the ground, their meaning only clear to him. Spiral's legs are thrown out in front of her, and she kicks them back and forth, barely avoiding Plasma with every sweep. He barely notices them, instead biting his nails, his eyes focusing on some far away spot beyond the cell walls.

"You know, you're so lucky," Spiral says, turning to Purple. "You just got in here and you're already about to get out."

Purple, who'd been lying across from her on the other side of the cell, props himself up and laughs. "You do realize I'm the only reason you're getting out of here in the first place."

"No, we had a plan. We had Raymond."

"What was Raymond going to do?"

"He was going to steal power from the guards. Take their power and use it." Spiral says. At Purple's apparent confusion, Raymond interjects, bringing up his hand and shielding it from the cameras. There's a small click and a mesh appears on the pad of his pointer finger.

"I've been siphoning a small fraction of a guard's power every time they've visited our cell."

"They didn't search you for implants?" Purple asks.

Raymond smiles, and the mesh disappears. He lets his hand drop. "They did. I'd barely call this an implant though. It slipped right by them. I'm a bit of a vacuum for power."

"Other people's power? And it doesn't kill you?" Purple says.

Plasma sits up and smiles. "Like I said. You aren't the only special one here."

Purple rolls his eyes. "Sure. Let's say you can steal another person's power without poisoning yourself. What have you stolen?"

"Control."

Purple blinks. "And… what are you going to do with that."

Raymond's smile falters, and he leans back onto his hands, not meeting Purple's eyes. "I don't know what it does. I only have enough of the power to use it once."

Purple laughs. "God no wonder you haven't made it out of here."

"Hey."

The air stiffens as Raymond glares at Purple. His hand twitches, glowing a slight green, before the light goes out. Purple notices, and his posture straightens. He brings his hands out in front of him, and they begin to glow their own light blue. A challenge. There's a stillness.

"Purple." Plasma lunges forward, knocking him towards the ground. "Cameras." He hisses, slamming a knee down on Purple's hands, concealing them from view. "Stop. Outputting. Now."

After a moment, Purple relaxes, and the glow dims from his hands. "Alright. Alright get off of me."

One more moment, and Plasma steps back, returning to where he'd been sitting. "We're never going to get out of here if we all kill each other."

"He lit up first," Purple says.

"Save your power for actually getting us out of here." Plasma says.

"I have. I think I've had enough for about a day now." Purple says.

Spiral sits up, glaring at him. "You're telling me for a whole day you've just been sitting on our escape route?"

"I wanted to play it safe! If we only have one shot it's better to have too much power than not enough."

Spiral stands, gesturing towards the door. "Ok. So shoot your laser at the wall. Get us out of here."

"I assume we're going to need some sort of plan." Oshin replies.

"We have a plan. He's got a laser." Spiral says.

Oshin rolls his eyes. "I assume there's going to be more to that."

"There's definitely going to be more than that." Plasma says, taking a seat. His pointer finger traces a circle on the ground. "The prison's a giant circle-"

Purple pushes himself to his feet. "Borringgggggggg." His hands begin to glow a bright blue once again, but this time it's brilliant, lighting up the cell in its entirety.

Above them, there's a loud crackling, and a robotic voice floods the room. "Halt what you're doing immediately. Any…" But it's soon drowned out by the louder hum coming from Purple. He braces himself as the light gathering between his palms begins to exert an almost physical pressure, pushing him back. Plasma mouth opens, but his words are drowned out as well. He's gesturing towards the ground, towards the ceiling, but his message goes unnoticed. The others all back away from Purple, pressing their bodies against the wall as he begins to radiate a sharp heat. It grows hotter, the light grows brighter, Plasma's shouting, Spiral's cheering, the chaos splinters the once dark cell into a cacophony of noise.

And then with an exhale, Purple pushes. In a blink the beam blitzes forward, slamming into the cell wall with full force. There's a moment of resistance. The wall itself glows a brilliant blue as it attempts to absorb the pure power. The beam dims, and for a horrifying moment it seemed like the cell wall was winning. That it'd absorb the power and disperse it. That in a few moments guards would burst into the room and slaughter them all. But with one final hiss, the wall gave way. The beam shot forward, leaving behind a perfect four foot hole in the dense plating, the edges tinged reddish-white with heat.

Plasma steps forward. "Ok now-"

But with a yell, Spiral's already flung herself headfirst through the hole. She curls, her shoulder absorbing the impact from the fall before popping back to her feet. "Let's fucking GO." She screams, before turning to her right and disappearing down the hallway.

"Wait-" Plasma shouts, but the other three have already thrown themselves forward, each passing through the hole with varying levels of grace. Plasma lurches forward, pulling himself through the hole after them. He turns to look back at the cell, where they'd spent what had felt like years. It looked unbearably small now, it looked unbearably dark.

Purple sprints to catch up to Spiral, who's almost made it to an imposing door at the end of the hallway. She looks behind her and sees him catching up. "Can you get us through that?" She says, "You better have enough to get us through that."

Purple smiles, and once again the bright light begins to pool between his fingers. "I barely used half."

"Wait." Plasma says, bringing himself to a stop. His voice strains to be heard over the charging blast of energy. "The layout of the prison, I wasn't able to tell you–"

But his words come out a fraction too late, and the beam slices forwards towards the door. Unlike with the wall, the paltry steel stands no chance at absorbing the power, and after enduring the blast for only a second, there's a horrific screech as the door is blasted off its hinges.

Raymond charges forward with a shout, determined to be the first out, but as he leaps into the cloud of smoke, a shout of terror is torn from his lips. His feet, reaching forward expecting ground come up empty handed, and he gives one desperate attempt, throwing out his hand as he plunges downward.

A hand reaches out and seizes hold of him, stopping his rapid descent. Raymond grabs onto the offered wrist tightly, and with a heave, Plasma pulls Raymond back onto the platform. The rest of the group crowds forward, staring at the scene in front of them.

Plasma rubs his arm, where the imprints from Raymond's grip are clearly visible. "What I was going to say." he says, through gritted teeth, "Is that VoidGate isn't built like the other prisons in Power City."

In front of the group, past the ragged hinges where the door once was, stretches an empty expanse. Steel cubes hover in the air around them, their size apparent even from a distance. Catwalks litter the space between them, some stationary, others turning idly. Oshin looks down, as his nose catches a whiff of briny air. Far below them, just barely in sight, seethes the dark blue, almost black ocean. Specks of white appear as waves come crashing upon each other.

"Cubes." Spiral says insightfully.

"Cell blocks," Plasma replies. "Like the one we're in now. The easiest way to prevent an escape. Hover us hundreds of feet above the ocean, isolated."

Raymond's eyes remain trained on the empty abyss below him, where he'd almost plummeted. His hand presses firmly against the wall. "So what do we do…" he asks.

There's a shout. The five look up to see catwalks suddenly moving with purpose, shifting in place. In the distance, partially obscured by the salty mist, the neon glow of VoidGuard uniforms shines through the night. Catwalks click into place as the unit approaches. Closer, closer.

"Shit– DUCK." Oshin shouts, and there's the terrifying hum of an energy blast as the five throw themselves to the ground.
